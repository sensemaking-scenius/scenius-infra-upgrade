Collaborative tldraw whiteboard from the Feb 25 Scenius Workshop, capturing the brainstorming session on Telegram group restructuring using the SCQOD framework.

## Problem Definition

Telegram as backplane (like Slack) — exploring chat/rooms alternatives and a new TG design.

## Proposed TG Structure

**Home = 4 primary TG channels:**
- General "chat"
- Bulletin Board (from sub-groups)
- Events
- The Drip — roll-ups from sub-groups

With sub-groups for each topic/working group. Members encouraged to join 2-5 groups.

**Problems being solved:**
- Overwhelm of info/content (chaos)
- Difficulty navigating content, especially as a new member
- Lack of member ownership over spaces

## Tensions

- Telegram is working (kinda?)
- Notion feels hard to work with
- Privacy concerns — private by default
- Awareness of event happenings
- Confusion on which channel to do things in
- Too much info overwhelm

## Options Considered

- Moving to a new community platform (not enough buy-in, maybe too big a change)
- Streamlining what we have
- Experimental app
- Opt-in subgroups with rollup summaries

## Sensemaking Scenius Structure

- **Supergroup** — group with topics, private by default, invites
- **Group** — private, opt-in by members-only, topic-centric areas
- **Channel** — public, anyone can join (e.g. "Sensemaking Drip") with de-identified data sharing or attributed content with approval

## Scenius 2nd Brain
